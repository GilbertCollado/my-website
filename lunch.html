Dominican Lunch Dishes

Lunch is the most important meal of the day in the Dominican Republic — rich, hearty, and full of tradition. Here are some favorites:

Popular Lunch Dishes

  • La Bandera – “The Flag”: rice, beans, and stewed meat, often served with salad and fried plantains.
  • Pollo Guisado – Dominican-style stewed chicken with bell peppers, garlic, and tomatoes.
  • Arroz con Fideos – Toasted noodles cooked with rice for added flavor and texture.
  • Pastelón – A Dominican lasagna made with sweet plantains and ground beef.

Lunch is the most important meal of the day in the Dominican Republic. Schools and workplaces often take extended lunch breaks so people can eat with their families.

La Bandera, the national dish, is named for its resemblance to the Dominican flag — white rice, red beans, and stewed meat. It's usually accompanied by salad and fried plantains.

Lunch is about nourishment and togetherness, and many dishes are designed to feed large groups using affordable, accessible ingredients.
