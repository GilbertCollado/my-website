Top 10 Dominican Dinner Dishes

Hearty and soulful meals from the DR

  1. Sancocho (7-Meat Stew)

    A rich, soul-warming stew made with up to seven meats and root vegetables—served with rice and avocado. A symbol of Dominican hospitality. :contentReference[oaicite:1]{index=1}

  2. Arroz con Pollo / Locrio de Pollo

    Comforting one-pot chicken and rice dish, rich in vegetables and sazón—popular for family dinners. :contentReference[oaicite:2]{index=2}

  3. Mofongo

    Mashed green plantains with garlic and chicharrón—served with broth or meat. A cultural cross-over from Puerto Rico to the DR. :contentReference[oaicite:3]{index=3}

  4. Pollo Guisado

    A staple of Dominican households: tender chicken stewed in tomato, garlic, oregano, and veggies. :contentReference[oaicite:4]{index=4}

  5. Moro de Guandules

    Rice cooked with pigeon peas, sofrito, and spices—a flavorful side or main dish. :contentReference[oaicite:5]{index=5}

  6. Sopa de Mondongo

    Hearty beef-tripe soup with vegetables—common in restaurants and Sunday dinners. :contentReference[oaicite:6]{index=6}

  7. Pernil

    Slow-roasted, marinated pork leg—especially popular during holidays like Christmas. :contentReference[oaicite:7]{index=7}

  8. Pastelón de Plátano Maduro

    A layered casserole of sweet plantains and ground meat—like Dominican lasagna. :contentReference[oaicite:8]{index=8}

  9. Pescado Frito

    Beach-style fried fish, served with rice, tostones, and salad. :contentReference[oaicite:9]{index=9}

  10. Chivo Guisado
